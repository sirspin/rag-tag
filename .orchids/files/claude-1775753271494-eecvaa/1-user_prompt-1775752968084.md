Ragtag — Landing Page Rebuild Prompt
Rebuild the landing page only. Do not touch any other routes, auth, schema, APIs, or backend logic.

Product Context
Ragtag is a shared newspaper platform for micronetworks. It takes the pressure off individuals to produce content — instead, a small group curates links and the paper organizes itself. The owner sets up the paper and invites staff reporters. Nobody has to write anything. The output is a beautiful, continuously updated newspaper.
This context should inform tone throughout — not a productivity tool, not a social network, not a newsletter platform. A shared newspaper for people who read.

Page Structure
1. Hero
Opening line:
"The web is full of things worth reading. Your group chat is full of links nobody reads."
Subhead with cycling word (animate through: mom, book, run, film, brunch — cycle on a timer, smooth transition):
"Ragtag is a shared newspaper for your [cycling word] club."
CTA button: "Start your paper →"

2. How It Works
Three equal columns, simple and clean:
1. Create a Paper
Become the editor. Set the tone. Invite your newsroom.
2. Open Submissions
File links and stories from anywhere. The paper organizes itself.
3. Read and Share
Visit your paper anytime. Your group's finds, plus recommended reads, in one place.

3. Example Papers
Placeholder section for now. No static mockup. Just a clean placeholder block that says something like "See it in action — example papers coming soon." Leave obvious room for real paper examples to be cycled through here eventually. Keep it minimal — don't design around content that doesn't exist yet.

4. Footer CTA
"Start your paper →" repeated. Clean, no friction.

Design Direction
Same typographic system as the rest of the site — Quattrocento and Arvo, cream background, Signal red accent. The landing page should feel like the cover of the product, not a marketing site. Editorial, warm, confident. Not a SaaS landing page.
No hero image. No illustration. Type does the work.
The cycling word animation should be subtle — not a flashy swap, more like a gentle fade or slide. The word changes, everything else stays still.

Tone
Editorial. Dry. Warm without being precious. No startup language — no "seamless", "powerful", "platform", "leverage", "streamline". Write like the product would write.

Do Not Touch
Everything except the landing page (/ route). All other routes, logic, schema, auth, and backend untouched.
