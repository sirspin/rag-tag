# Ragtag — V2 Product Redirect Prompt

This is a product direction change, not a polish pass. Preserve all existing auth, schema foundations, and infrastructure. The UI and product concept need to shift significantly.

-----

## What Ragtag Is Now

Ragtag is a living newspaper that a group populates continuously. Not a weekly compilation tool — a site that updates as links come in, readable anytime, owned by one person, contributed to by optional staff reporters.

The owner pays. Staff reporters are free. The paper works as a solo product (personal reading log with a beautiful output) or as a group product (a newsroom with contributors). Both are valid use cases.

-----

## Core Concept Changes

### From weekly edition → living site

- The paper is always current. Links submitted today appear today.
- Stories are organized by the AI into sections on an ongoing basis, not compiled once a week.
- A weekly digest email is an optional toggle the owner can turn on or off. When on, it sends a compilation of that week’s stories. When off, nothing goes out — the site is the product.

### From contributors → staff reporters

- People who join a paper are staff reporters, not contributors.
- Each reporter can optionally set a role title (anything — “Calzone and Pizza Critic”, “Foreign Correspondent”, “The Sports Desk”) and a short bio.
- The role and bio appear on their byline in the paper. If no role is set, just their display name.
- Roles are purely expressive — no functional difference between roles.

### Style system — gesture only, don’t build yet

- The paper will eventually support different editorial styles that transform how submitted content is headlined and framed. Different styles will have creative names, not outlet names.
- For now: build a style field on the paper settings page with a single placeholder option called “Standard.” The architecture should make adding styles later straightforward.
- Do not build the AI style transformation yet. Just the field and the placeholder.

### Submission methods

- Web submission page (existing, keep)
- SMS input: each paper gets a dedicated Twilio number. Texting a link to that number submits it to the paper. Owner sees the number in their dashboard.
- Email input: each paper gets a dedicated email address (e.g. schmo-bros@ragtag.is). Forwarding or emailing a link submits it to the paper.
- All three input methods feed the same submissions pipeline.

## Paper Structure

### Owner

- Creates the paper, sets the name, tagline, style
- Pays the subscription
- Can invite staff reporters
- Controls weekly digest toggle
- Has a role and bio too if they want one

### Staff Reporters

- Invited by owner
- Set their own role title and bio (optional)
- Submit links via web, SMS, or email
- Free, no payment required

### The Paper

- Lives at `/p/[slug]` — always current, publicly readable
- Organized into sections by AI on an ongoing basis
- Each story shows: headline (from OG), contributor note if provided, byline with role, source name, extracted text or OG description, read link
- No AI editorializing — no section ledes, no recommendation reason lines. AI organizes, humans speak.

## Pages to Update

### `/dashboard`

- Show paper’s SMS number and email address prominently
- Show digest toggle (on/off)
- Show style selector (placeholder “Standard” only for now)
- Staff reporter list with their roles visible

### `/paper/[slug]` (the living paper)

- Stories flow in reverse chronological order within sections
- AI re-organizes sections as new submissions arrive
- No “publish” button — submissions appear as they come in
- Byline shows: `[Display Name], [Role Title]` if role set, just `[Display Name]` if not

### `/paper/[slug]/staff` (new)

- Simple page showing all staff reporters, their roles, their bios
- The masthead page essentially — who makes this paper

### `/invite/[token]`

- Update copy: “You’ve been invited to join [Paper Name] as a staff reporter.”
- After claiming: prompt for role title and bio before landing on submission page. Both optional, skippable.

## Copy/Tone Notes

- Owner = the person who runs the paper
- Staff Reporter = anyone who contributes
- Never “user”, “contributor”, “content”
- The paper’s SMS number: “File a story” not “submit a link”
- Digest toggle label: “Weekly edition” not “email digest”
